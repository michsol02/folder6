THE R100 BADGE

Businesses that actively and continually seek to balance their pursuit of profit and business excellence, with the needs of people and planet, need due recognition and support.

By providing real choice for consumers, employees and investors who want to help create and be part of a better kind of business, we create economic reward for pursuing real responsibility. As well as creating the critical economic incentives for other businesses to follow suit.

By connecting the businesses that are best for the world with all those people who wish to see them succeed, we can make real responsibility an increasingly powerful driver of business success.

Responsible 100 is exploring various ways in which participating businesses can publicise their participation. A badge or kitemark, alongside the provision of statements of commitment to critical issues, in the form of assets for publication are a distinct possibility.

ROUNDTABLES

Responsible 100’s unique collaborative approach to business responsibility is rooted in efforts to bring a wide range of actors and critical friends together. Namely, businesses and their various stakeholders. 

 We held our first roundtable peer review in 2013 and convened a total of 57 over the following five years. 

These roundtable events brought people together, face to face, in ways that are not possible today. But through the smart use of technology and process design we’ll continue to enable many of the good things to continue to happen.

That is to:

  1. Discuss and debate a wide range of social, environmental, ethical and governance issues.
  2. Bring together diverse groups of individuals - including business practitioners, trade bodies, campaigners and NGOs, academics and government 
  3. Enable frank, constructive dialogue on the responsibility issues affecting business and wider society 
  4. Focus on collaboration and co-creation.  Learn from experts and practitioners alike - people who share a passion for the issues under examination and on continuously improving policies and practices to address them 
  5. Share and activate knowledge and views as to latest thinking, expertise and developments 
  6. Network with peers, experts, practitioners and those affected by the actions (and sometimes inactions) of business 
  7. Discuss latest best practices and latest thinking, innovations, likely directions of travel, and more. 
  8. Generate new ideas and innovations, and practical changes that you can make in your business to improve 
  9. Deploy imagination as a driver of business excellence and contribute to a deeper understanding as to good, better and best practices on any given issue
